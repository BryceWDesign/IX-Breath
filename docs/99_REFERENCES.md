# IX-Breath References

This file lists the public technical references used to bound IX-Breath.

The purpose of this file is not to imply endorsement.
The purpose is to keep architectural claims tied to visible, reviewable source material.

---

## R1 — NASA Exploration EVA System Concept of Operations

**Title:** EVA-EXP-0042 Exploration EVA System Concept of Operations  
**Organization:** NASA  
**Why it matters:** Public xEVA duration and contingency baseline; informs the survival-gap problem IX-Breath is trying to address.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** EVAs up to 8 hours, with an additional 1 hour of secondary/backup oxygen for off-nominal operations.  
**URL:** https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/eva-exp-0042_xeva_system_con_ops_rev_b_final_dtd_10192020_ref_doc.pdf

---

## R2 — NASA PLSS Roadmap

**Title:** Primary Life Support System (PLSS) Roadmap, ICES-2025-61  
**Organization:** NASA / NTRS  
**Why it matters:** Public packaging and integration constraints for PLSS-class systems.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** Suit backpack design is constrained by volume, on-back mass, center of gravity, and front-to-back dimension.  
**URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/20250004024/downloads/ICES-2025-61.pdf

---

## R3 — NASA ECLSS Overview

**Title:** Environmental Control and Life Support Systems (ECLSS)  
**Organization:** NASA  
**Why it matters:** Public baseline for oxygen generation, water recovery, and atmospheric control.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** NASA publicly describes oxygen generation, water recovery, and air revitalization as integrated life-support functions.  
**URL:** https://www.nasa.gov/reference/environmental-control-and-life-support-systems-eclss/

---

## R4 — NASA RCA Development

**Title:** Rapid Cycle Amine (RCA 2.0) System Development  
**Organization:** NASA / NTRS  
**Why it matters:** Public basis for suit-scale, low-power CO2 and humidity control using alternating amine beds.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** RCA is a low-power assembly capable of simultaneously removing carbon dioxide and humidity and regenerating when exposed to vacuum.  
**URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/20130013106/downloads/20130013106.pdf

## R5 — NASA SWME Development

**Title:** Spacesuit Water Membrane Evaporator Development for Advanced Spacesuits  
**Organization:** NASA / NTRS  
**Why it matters:** Public basis for suit-scale thermal control and water-linked heat rejection.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** SWME is a public NASA technical lane for advanced spacesuit thermal control.

## R6 — NASA Space Power State of the Art

**Title:** Power Subsystems — NASA Small Spacecraft State of the Art  
**Organization:** NASA  
**Why it matters:** Public baseline for current space solar and storage practice.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** Public NASA material describes >32% efficient solar cells in space power applications.  
**URL:** https://www.nasa.gov/smallsat-institute/sst-soa/power-subsystems/

## R7 — NASA ECLSS Factsheet

**Title:** Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) Factsheet  
**Organization:** NASA  
**Why it matters:** Supplemental public statement of oxygen generation by electrolysis and water recovery.  
**Key public takeaway used in IX-Breath:** NASA publicly states that oxygen on the ISS is produced by electrolysis of water using electrical power from solar arrays.  
**URL:** https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/g-281237_eclss_0.pdf

## Reference use policy

IX-Breath uses these references to:

- bound problem framing,
- constrain claims,
- justify subsystem lanes,
- define validation expectations.

IX-Breath does **not** use these references to imply:

- flight qualification,
- agency endorsement,
- standards compliance by citation alone,
- automatic transferability from station-scale systems to backpack-scale systems.

Every scaling step still has to be earned by analysis and validation.
